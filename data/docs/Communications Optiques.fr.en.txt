              Teaching Unit: Wide Area Network Technologies and Protocols
ECUE no 3.1: Optical Communications
Constituent Element Plan (ECUE)
Prerequisites:
 
1- General knowledge in geometric optics
2- Knowledge of waves and propagation
3- Knowledge of signal processing tools (Fourier transform, probabilities, etc.)
 
Constituent Element Learning Objectives (CELU):
 
OE1- Generalize the different elements of the optical communication system.
OE2- Demonstrate the fundamental concepts associated with the design, analysis and testing of
performance of fiber optic transmission systems and optical networks
OE3- Evaluate the main components of an optical communication system
OE4- Organize and structure optical networks

Description of the content of the constituent element
Chapter 1: Introduction to guided optics  
1.1 Motivations and history  
1.2 Basic principle of optical guidance  
1.2 Optical guide and optical fiber  
1.3 Manufacturing technology
Chapter 2: Optical fiber.
2.1 Guiding principle from ray theory  
2.2 Concept of numerical aperture and bandwidth
2.3 Multimode Fiber Specification Sheet Study  
2.4 Principle of guidance from the approach of plane waves  
2.5 Concept of modes and dispersion equation  
2.6 Single-mode fiber specification sheet study
Chapter 3: Losses and attenuation of an optical guide  
3.1 Coupling losses of an optical guide
3.2 Connection between two guides (Gluing, connectors, splices)
3.3 Propagation losses,
3.4 Curvature losses
Chapter 4: Optical Transmitters and Receivers
4.1 Light-emitting diodes
4.2 LASER diodes
4.3 Photodiodes  
4.5 Phototransistors  
Chapter 5: Multiplexing Techniques  
5.1 Optical Time Division Multiplexing (OTDM)
5.2 Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM)
5.3 Optical Code Division Multiplexing (OCDMA)
Chapter 6: Study of some optical components  
6.1 The splitter
6.2 The type  
6.3 The multiplexer
