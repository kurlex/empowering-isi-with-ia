Teaching Unit: Mobile and Wireless Networks
ECUE no 1.2: Wireless network technologies and Internet of Things
Constituent Element Plan (ECUE)
Prerequisites:
 
1- Basic concepts of radio propagation,  
2- basic notions of networks addressing, routing, local networks, physical layer, layer
MAC
 
 
Constituent Element Learning Objectives (CELU):
 
At the end of the component, the student will be able to:
OE1: Learn about the different radio communications technologies that operate
mainly in ISM bands (unlicensed): WPAN, WLAN, WMAN
OE2: Know the characteristics of Manets, Vanets and RCSFs networks
OE3: Learn about the different radio protocols proposed for the Internet of Things

Description of the content of the constituent element
Specify the chapters and their subsections by adopting the organization of a reference book
known and referred in the field. Also specifies the number of hours of courses (C), CI, TD,
TP, project or other necessary for each chapter or part.
Chapter 1: Wireless Radio Networks: Basic Characteristics and Standards  
1. Reminders of the characteristics of radio propagation: path loss, gain, antenna, multi-paths,
fading...  
2. Wireless radio networks: classification, standards and norms, flow rate and range  
3. Case studies:  
3.1 WPAN and the Bluetooth standard (IEEE802.15.1): frequency bands, protocols (L2CAP, SDP, …),
piconet, scatternet  
3.2 WLAN and WIFI standard (IEEE 802.11): physical layer (FHSS, DSSS, OFDM), MAC layer (modes
pcf and dcf access, frame format, association procedure, handover, security), variants of the
standard (a/c/e/f/g/h/i/j/s/t/u/v/w) 3.3 WMAN and the Wimax standard (IEEE802.16): range, frequencies,
LoS/Nlos transmissions  
Chapter 2: Mobility in Wireless Radio Networks  
1. Mobile ad hoc networks (Manets)  
1.1 Addressing/identification of wireless equipment  
1.2 Interference management 1.3 Routing algorithms: proactive, reactive and hybrid  
2. The Vanets  
2.1 ITS and V2V communications  
2.2 The WAVE standard for Vanets (IEEE 802.11 p)  
2.3 Routing, QoS and security in VANETs  
Chapter 3: Wireless Sensor Networks (WSNs)  
1. What is a sensor node?  
1.1 Anatomy and examples of sensor nodes  
1.2 Protocol stack  
1.3 Constraints of miniaturization: energy, memory, processor  
1.4 Applications of sensor networks  
2. Protocols and standards for RCSFs  
2.1 The Zigbbee alliance, Zigbee/IEEE 802.15.4 architecture, frame format, access mode: Beaconing
and GTS, Topologies (star, Mesh, hierarchical)  
2.2 BANs and the IEEE 802.15.6 standard  
2.3 IPv6 adaptation layers (6LowPAN, 6tish)  
2.4 Routing protocols: classification (topology/paradigm/application), the RPL 132 Standard  
Chapter 4: Radio Communications for the Internet of Things  
1. Concept of object and IoT protocol stack  
2. Radio protocols for IoT  
2.1 Near-field communications & radio tag identification: RFID and NFC  
2.2 WPANs for IoT: BLE and Zigbee 3.0  
2.3 WLANs for IoT: IEEE802.11ah and IEEE802.11af (cognitive radio for IoT)  
2.4 LPWAs: LoRaWAN (LoRa modulation, characteristics: duty cycle, spreading factor, coding rate,
LoRAMAC, LoRa security), Sigfox  
2.5 The Nb-IoT mobile solution  
3. IoT Applications: Case Studies  
3.1 Smart city application  
3.2 Healthcare application  
3.3 IoT and Industry 4.0  
Practical work & tutorials  
 Omnet TPs for the simulation of wireless networks and sensor networks  
 TP: Simpy for the simulation of the LoRaWAN network  
 Practical work is also planned in this module.
